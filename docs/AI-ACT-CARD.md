# DeepAsk AI Act transparency card

This is not legal advice. It is the product's current operational self-assessment for demo and prototype use.

## Product

DeepAsk Live is an adaptive survey and civic listening prototype.

It:

- asks participants survey questions
- uses AI to generate neutral follow-up questions
- uses AI to draft summaries
- lets participants confirm or edit the summary
- stores original answers separately from AI interpretation
- aggregates completed responses into Mind Hive group patterns

## Current AI role

AI is used for:

- clarification
- summarisation
- grouping signals
- drafting possible next questions

AI is not used for:

- legal decisions
- financial decisions
- employment decisions
- education access or scoring
- welfare or public-benefit access
- migration/asylum/border decisions
- law-enforcement decisions
- biometric identification or categorisation
- ranking individual people

## Working classification

Current prototype classification:

**Transparency / limited-risk AI use**

Reason:

- users interact with an AI-assisted flow and should be informed that AI is used
- generated summaries are interpretation and must be labelled as such
- participants can confirm or edit summaries
- organisers remain responsible for human review
- the system is designed for civic feedback, not consequential decisions about individuals

## Boundary condition

DeepAsk must be reassessed before deployment if it is used in a context where outputs influence access to:

- employment
- education
- credit
- housing
- welfare or essential public services
- migration/asylum/border control
- law-enforcement treatment
- democratic or judicial decisions

Those contexts may trigger high-risk analysis under the EU AI Act.

## Safeguards already present

- Participant-facing explanation of AI use
- Warning not to include sensitive personal information
- Original answer text kept separate from AI summaries
- Participant can confirm or edit final summary
- Public Mind Hive shows group patterns, not individual raw answers
- Organiser audit is protected by organiser code
- AI is instructed not to persuade, judge, profile, or decide

## Minimum public wording

DeepAsk uses AI to ask neutral follow-up questions and draft summaries. AI output is an interpretation, not a decision. Participants can confirm or edit summaries before submission. Organisers remain responsible for human review.
